# The "Do" List

1. Audit your privacy settings regularly: Ensure you know exactly who can see your posts, tagged photos, and personal information.

2. Build a professional presence: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry professionals.

3. Think before you post: Use the "Billboard Test"—if you wouldn't want it on a giant sign in the middle of campus, don't post it.

4. Showcase your wins: Share your academic projects, certifications, or volunteer work to create a positive digital footprint.

5. Verify information: Fact-check news or campus updates before sharing them to avoid spreading misinformation.

# The "Don't" List

1. Don't post in the heat of the moment: Avoid venting about professors, classes, or classmates when you are angry or frustrated.

2. Don't share sensitive data: Never post your student ID number, exact dorm location, or real-time travel plans.

3. Don't assume "Private" means "Permanent": Screenshots and archives mean that anything you post can potentially be seen by anyone, forever.

4. Don't engage with trolls: Avoid getting into public online arguments or participating in any form of cyberbullying.

5. Don't ignore the Code of Conduct: Avoid posting content that shows illegal activities or violations of your university’s policies.
