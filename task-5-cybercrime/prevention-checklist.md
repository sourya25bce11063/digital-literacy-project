# The "Stay Safe Online" Checklist for Students

1. Secure Your Accounts with MFA: Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your college portals, Gmail, and Instagram. Even if someone gets your password, they won't be able to log in without the secondary code.

2. Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Delete "loan apps" or "gaming apps" that ask for unnecessary access to your gallery or contact list.

3. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank account or college student portal using free canteen or railway station Wi-Fi. If you must use it, use a trusted VPN.

4. Practice "Link Hygiene": Do not click on links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming you’ve won a scholarship, a free laptop, or that your SIM card/electricity will be blocked. These are classic phishing tactics.

5. Verify Social Media Identities: Before accepting a follow request from a "classmate" or "senior," verify their identity in person. Scammers often clone profiles to ask for emergency money or to harass students.

6. Check for "HTTPS": Before entering any personal data on a website (like for exam registrations or internship applications), ensure the URL starts with https:// and has a padlock icon.

7. UPI Safety: No PIN for Receiving Money: Always remember that you do not need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a "Collect Request" or a QR code claiming it's to "verify" a payment to you, it is a scam.

8. UPI Safety: Set Transaction Limits: Use your banking app to set a daily limit on UPI transactions. This ensures that even if your phone is compromised, the potential financial loss is capped.

• National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file a formal complaint.

• National Helpline Number: Dial 1930 immediately.
