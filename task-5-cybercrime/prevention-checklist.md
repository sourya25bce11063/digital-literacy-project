# The "Stay Safe Online" Checklist for Students

• Secure Your Accounts with MFA: Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your college portals, Gmail, and Instagram. Even if someone gets your password, they won't be able to log in without the secondary code.

• Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Delete "loan apps" or "gaming apps" that ask for unnecessary access to your gallery or contact list.

• Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank account or college student portal using free canteen or railway station Wi-Fi. If you must use it, use a trusted VPN.

• Practice "Link Hygiene": Do not click on links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming you’ve won a scholarship, a free laptop, or that your SIM card/electricity will be blocked. These are classic phishing tactics.

• Verify Social Media Identities: Before accepting a follow request from a "classmate" or "senior," verify their identity in person. Scammers often clone profiles to ask for emergency money or to harass students.

• Check for "HTTPS": Before entering any personal data on a website (like for exam registrations or internship applications), ensure the URL starts with https:// and has a padlock icon.

• UPI Safety: No PIN for Receiving Money: Always remember that you do not need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a "Collect Request" or a QR code claiming it's to "verify" a payment to you, it is a scam.

• UPI Safety: Set Transaction Limits: Use your banking app to set a daily limit on UPI transactions. This ensures that even if your phone is compromised, the potential financial loss is capped.

• Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your laptop and smartphone OS. These updates often contain "security patches" that fix vulnerabilities exploited by hackers.

• Be Wary of "Digital Arrest" Threats: If you receive a video call from someone claiming to be from the CBI or Police saying your "parcel" has illegal items, hang up immediately. Real officials never "arrest" people over video calls.

• National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file a formal complaint.

• National Helpline Number: Dial 1930 immediately.
